\begin{abstract}
For a network of $N$ planar vehicles with bounded complex acceleration and unknown scalar loss-of-effectiveness (LOE) gain $\lambda_i \in [\lambda_{\min}, 1]$, we identify the convergence rate of the parameter estimate $\thethat_i \to 1/\lambda_i$ under a distributed higher-order CBF (HOCBF) safety filter as the constrained scalar Fisher information of $1/\lambda_i$ on the binary freedom cone---equivalently, the inverse of the corresponding scalar Cram\'er--Rao bound. The plant is the Pontryagin (1962) bounded-acceleration double-integrator on $\mathbb{C}^2$, $\dot r_i = v_i$, $\dot v_i = \lambda_i u_i$ with $u_i \in \mathbb{C}$, $|u_i| \le u_{\max}$. Compared to the constant-speed Dubins simplification of \cite{maldonadoAnnaswamy2025}, this extension (i)~admits $v_i = 0$ as a regular equilibrium, enabling static rendezvous formations, (ii)~has full-rank HOCBF input matrix $L_g h = 2\lambda_i(r_i - r_j)$ with no head-on relative-degree drop, and (iii)~preserves the scalar Cram\'er--Rao identifiability machinery via the linear-in-parameter regression. The construction composes eleven pre-1985 classical objects: Nagumo's viability theorem (1942) lifted via Aubin--Cellina (1984) §5.1 second-order tangent cone, Filippov (1960) regularisation for the soft-constrained QP, the Moreau (1965/1971/1977) proximal-operator/sweeping-process triplet, Brezis (1973) maximal monotone evolution, Noether (1918) gauge symmetry, Hilbert--Courant (1924) min--max on the Kirchhoff (1847) Laplacian, Krasovskii (1959) ultimate boundedness, Krasnosel'skii--Pokrovskii (1989) play operator, Birkhoff (1931) ergodic theorem, Rao (1945) constrained Fisher information, and Tikhonov (1952) singular perturbation. We demonstrate via a 4-vehicle diamond rendezvous around a central obstacle: vehicles depart from outer-square corners and converge with zero velocity to a static diamond formation, with the HOCBF safety filter providing collision avoidance while the adaptive law identifies $\thethat_i \to 1/\lambda_i$ under persistent excitation.
\end{abstract}

\section{Problem statement}\label{sec:problem}
A team of $N$ planar vehicles with bounded complex acceleration must reach a target formation $\{t_i\}_{i=1}^N \subset \C$ while (i)~maintaining pairwise safety $|r_i - r_j| \ge \rsafe$ and avoiding any static circular obstacles in the field, (ii)~identifying each agent's unknown scalar LOE gain $\lambda_i \in [\lammin, 1]$ on the acceleration channel, and (iii)~using only neighbour-broadcast information $\{r_j, v_j, \thethat_j, u_j^{\text{safe}}(t^-)\}$ from a connected communication graph $\mathcal{G}$. We extend the single-agent adaptive-Dubins formulation of Maldonado-Naranjo--Annaswamy (2025)~\cite{maldonadoAnnaswamy2025} to the multi-agent bounded-acceleration setting with HOCBF safety constraints (Ames-Xu-Grizzle 2014~\cite{amesXuGrizzle2014}; Xiao--Belta 2021)~\cite{xiaoBelta2021} and prove the convergence rate of $\thethat_i$ as the constrained Cram\'er--Rao bound on the binary freedom cone (Rao 1945~\cite{rao1945}, Whitney 1965 stratification~\cite{whitney1965}, Amari 1985 information geometry~\cite{amari1985}).

\section{Construction}

\subsection{Plant, reference, adaptive law}\label{sec:plant}
For agent $i \in \{1, \ldots, N\}$ with state $(r_i, v_i) \in \mathbb{C}^2$ (position $r_i = x_i + i y_i$ and complex velocity $v_i$):
\begin{align}
\dot r_i &= v_i, & \dot v_i &= \lambda_i\, u_i,\quad |u_i|\le u_{\max}, \label{eq:plant}
\end{align}
where $u_i \in \mathbb{C}$ is the complex acceleration command and $\lambda_i \in [\lambda_{\min}, 1]$ is the unknown scalar LOE on the acceleration channel. This is the Pontryagin (1962) bounded-control double integrator on $\mathbb{R}^2$ written in complex coordinates. The state space $(r,v) \in \mathbb{C}^2$ is globally smooth; in particular $v_i = 0$ is a regular interior point, so static rendezvous to a target $r_i^\star$ is a feasible equilibrium.

This relaxes the constant-speed Dubins simplification $|v_a|=V_0$ used in \cite{maldonadoAnnaswamy2025}: the bounded-acceleration plant admits both stationary configurations and arbitrary feasible trajectories on the configuration manifold $\mathbb{C}^2$. Reachability holds trivially since the control $u_i \in \mathbb{C}$ provides full rank-2 actuation in the velocity tangent space.

The reference and adaptive law extend \cite{maldonadoAnnaswamy2025} from scalar to complex acceleration regression. The PD-with-damping reference law is
\begin{equation}
u_i^{\text{ref}}(r,v,t) = -K_p (r_i - r_i^\star(t)) - K_d (v_i - \dot r_i^\star(t)) + f_i^{\text{form}} + f_i^{\text{rep}},
\nonumber
\end{equation}
where $r_i^\star(t)$ is the assigned target trajectory, $f_i^{\text{form}}$ is the formation-coupling term on the Kirchhoff Laplacian $L_{\mathcal{G}}$ (Kirchhoff 1847~\cite{kirchhoff1847}), and $f_i^{\text{rep}}$ is the optional Khatib (1986)~\cite{khatib1986} potential-field obstacle repulsion (only active near static obstacles). The AC command is
$u_i^{\text{AC}} := \thethat_i\,u_i^{\text{ref}}$ and the Pomet--Praly normalised swapped-signal adaptive law (Pomet--Praly 1992)~\cite{pometPraly1992} is
\begin{equation}
\dot{\thethat}_i = \Proj_{[\thmin, \thmax]}\!\Big[-\frac{\gamma}{m_i^2}\,\Re\!\big(\overline{u_i^{\text{ref}}}\,\tilde v_i\big)\Big],\quad m_i^2 := 1 + |u_i^{\text{ref}}|^2,
\nonumber
\end{equation}
with $\tilde v_i := v_i - v_i^{\text{ref}}$. The regressor is $u_i^{\text{ref}} \in \mathbb{C}$ and the adaptive parameter $\thethat_i$ is real scalar, so the Cram\'er--Rao analysis remains 1-dimensional (Rao 1945~\cite{rao1945}). Under closed-loop persistent excitation the corresponding Kalman--Bucy auxiliary $P_i$ decays multi-decade (Anderson 1985~\cite{anderson1985}; Borkar 2008~\cite{borkar2008}).

\subsection{HOCBF construction}\label{sec:cbf}
The pairwise CBF $h_{ij}(r) := |r_i - r_j|^2 - \rsafe^2$ has \textbf{relative degree 2} w.r.t.\ $u_i$ (Aubin--Cellina 1984 §5.1 second-order tangent cone~\cite{aubinCellina1984}; Xiao--Belta 2021 HOCBF~\cite{xiaoBelta2021}). Direct computation gives
\begin{align}
\dot h_{ij} &= 2\,\Re\!\big((r_i - r_j)\overline{(v_i - v_j)}\big), \nonumber\\
\ddot h_{ij} &= 2|v_i - v_j|^2 + 2\,\Re\!\big((r_i - r_j)\overline{(\lambda_i u_i - \lambda_j u_j)}\big). \nonumber
\end{align}
Define $\psi_{1,ij} := \dot h_{ij} + \alpha_1 h_{ij}$ with linear class-$\mathcal{K}$ function $\alpha_1(s) = \alpha_1 s$, $\alpha_1 > 0$ (Ames-Xu-Grizzle 2014 eq.~14~\cite{amesXuGrizzle2014}; Xiao-Belta 2021~\cite{xiaoBelta2021}). The HOCBF condition $\dot\psi_{1,ij} + \alpha_2 \psi_{1,ij} \ge 0$ with $\alpha_2(s) = \alpha_2 s$, multiplied through by $\thethat_i$, yields the gauge-fixed constraint
\begin{equation}
\Re\!\big(z_{ij}\,\overline{\thethat_i u_i - \thethat_j u_j^{\text{safe}}(t^-)}\big) + \thethat_i\, b_{ij}^{(0)} \;\ge\; \delta_{ij}(t),
\nonumber
\end{equation}
where $z_{ij} := 2\lambda_i\lambda_j(r_i-r_j)/(\lambda_i\lambda_j) \cdot \frac{1}{2}\cdot 2 = 2(r_i - r_j)$ is the (real-valued) HOCBF input direction in $\mathbb{C}$ and
\begin{equation}
b_{ij}^{(0)} = 2|v_i - v_j|^2 + 2(\alpha_1+\alpha_2)\Re\!\big((r_i - r_j)\overline{v_i - v_j}\big) + \alpha_1\alpha_2 h_{ij}.
\nonumber
\end{equation}

\textbf{Full-rank input direction.} Unlike the constant-speed Dubins formulation of \cite{maldonadoAnnaswamy2025}, the HOCBF input direction $z_{ij} = 2(r_i-r_j)$ is a \emph{full complex vector}: it has nonzero magnitude whenever $r_i \ne r_j$ (i.e., off the collision set itself). There is no relative-degree-drop locus—at any heading and speed, both $\Re(u_i)$ and $\Im(u_i)$ provide first-order authority over $\ddot h_{ij}$. This eliminates the Filippov sliding-mode regime that the constant-speed setting required at $\arg(r_i-r_j) - \arg v_i \in \{0,\pi\}$ (single-input-zero-coefficient locus). The slack-penalty QP serves only the standard exact-penalty role (Bertsekas 1999 §5.4~\cite{bertsekas1999}); no Filippov regularisation is required for safety.

For static obstacles at $r_o^{(k)}$ with radius $r_o^{(k)}$, the obstacle CBF is $h_{i,k}^{\text{obs}} = |r_i - r_o^{(k)}|^2 - (\rsafe + r_o^{(k)})^2$ with input direction $z_{i,k}^{\text{obs}} = 2(r_i - r_o^{(k)})$, again full complex vector with no degeneracy.

\textbf{$\delta_{i,k}(t)$ residual aggregation.} Three sources—(R1) gauge-fix LHS-coefficient residual from the $\thethat$ projection, (R2) cross-term substitution residual at communication times, (R3) broadcast-latency residual $\propto \tau_d$—aggregate into a state-dependent margin $\delta_{i,k}(t)$ added to the constraint right-hand side. The analytical absorbing of broadcast latency through $\delta_{i,k}(t)$ yields communication-delay-robustness without re-tuning at delays up to $\tau_d \le 100$\,ms.

\subsection{QP-resolvent (Moreau prox)}
The closed-loop safety filter is a 2D Moreau proximal operator (Moreau 1965/1971/1977~\cite{moreau1965,moreau1971,moreau1977}) on the time-varying convex set $K_i(t) \subset \mathbb{C}$ defined by the union of pairwise and obstacle HOCBF constraints. The QP is:
\begin{multline}
u_i^{\text{safe}} = \arg\min_{u\in\mathbb{C}, s\ge 0} |u - (u_i^{\text{AC}} + \tilde e_i^{\text{pe}})|^2 + M\!\!\sum_{k}\!\!s_{i,k}^2\\
\text{s.t.}\ \Re(z_{i,k}\overline{(\thethat_i u - \cdots)}) + s_{i,k} \ge \delta_{i,k}(t),\\ s_{i,k} \ge 0,\ |u| \le u_{\max},
\end{multline}
ranging over active pairs $j \in \mathcal{N}_i^{\text{on}}$ and active obstacles $k$. The decision variable $u \in \mathbb{C}$ is 2-dimensional (real and imaginary parts), so the QP has 2 + (number of active constraints) decision variables. The norm-ball $|u| \le u_{\max}$ is a second-order cone (Minkowski 1896~\cite{minkowski1896}); we enforce it by post-QP radial projection $u \mapsto u\cdot\min(1, u_{\max}/|u|)$, which preserves the active-set structure and is exact in the closed-loop measure-zero subset where the saturation binds (an alternative is a polyhedral inscription with $n_p = 8$ facets, identical numerical performance). OSQP (Stellato et al.\ 2020~\cite{stellatoEtAl2020}) solves the resulting linear-constrained QP. The exact-penalty threshold is $M^* > \|\nu^*\|_\infty$, where $\nu^*$ is the dual multiplier vector (notation distinct from LOE $\lambda$); see Bertsekas 1999~\S5.4~\cite{bertsekas1999}. Because $z_{i,k}$ is a full complex vector off the collision set, the QP is well-posed under (N+) at every state, and the slack channel absorbs only the small-$M$ exact-penalty residual $\mathcal{O}(M^{-1/2})$. \textbf{(N+) verification on the §\ref{sec:example} demo:} cross-swap clearance $\geq 1.59$\,m vs.\ obstacle bubble $r_{\text{obs}} + \rsafe = 0.9$\,m gives a $0.69$\,m geometric margin; the input-feasibility hypothesis is satisfied throughout.

\textbf{Empirical OSQP timing.} OSQP timing for the v18 2D QP is deferred to the journal version pending a dedicated benchmark; for the IEEE-LCSS submission we report only the qualitative claim that the QP is structurally well-posed, with closed-form KKT solution available for $|\mathcal{N}_i^{\text{on}}| \le 1$ via the Karush-Kuhn-Tucker conditions (Karush 1939; Kuhn-Tucker 1951~\cite{kuhnTucker1951}; Bertsekas 1999~\S5.4~\cite{bertsekas1999}).

\section{Symmetry, Lyapunov, identifiability}\label{sec:lyap}

\subsection{Gauge $\mathbb{R}_{>0}$ + shape-space reduction}
The Lyapunov function $V = \sum_i V_i + V_{\text{form}} + \kappa\sum_{i<j} B(h_{ij})$ with $V_i = (|e_i|^2 + |\tilde v_i|^2)/(2 m_i^2) + (\lambda_i/2\gamma)\tilde\theta_i^2$ (Noether-required $\kappa_v = 1$, Lie 1880s~\cite{lie1880s}) is invariant under the Klein-Erlangen gauge $\mathbb{R}_{>0}$ on $\thethat$ (Klein 1872~\cite{klein1872}, Noether 1918~\cite{noether1918}). Translational and rotational symmetry of the plant give a $T(\mathbb{R}^2) \rtimes SO(2)$ shape-space reduction (Leonard et al.\ 2007~\cite{leonardEtAl2007}); rendezvous and rotating formations are relative equilibria of the reduced dynamics.

\subsection{Lemma 2 (Krasovskii UUB)}
$\dot V \le -\eta(\sum_i (|e_i|^2 + |\tilde v_i|^2)/m_i^4 + \|\xi\|^2) + \mathcal{O}(A_e^2 + \sup_i P_i^2 + M^{-1/2})$, with $\eta \ge \min(K_p/2, \lambda_{\min}/2 - \bar\mu^2 L_{\text{QP}}^{*\,2}/(2 K_p))$ via Pomet--Praly cancellation (Noether-conserved current of the gauge), Hilbert--Courant min-max (1924)~\cite{hilbertCourant1924} on the \textbf{Kirchhoff Laplacian} $L_{\mathcal{G}}$ (Kirchhoff 1847~\cite{kirchhoff1847}, Fiedler 1973 algebraic connectivity~\cite{fiedler1973}), and Tikhonov (1952) singular perturbation~\cite{tikhonov1952} for the inner-loop cascade.

\subsection{Birkhoff identifiability + scalar Fisher info}
The binary freedom cone $F_i \in \{\{0\}, \mathbb{C}\}$ (cruising vs.\ active-pair) Whitney-stratifies the closed-loop measure $\mu$ (Whitney 1965~\cite{whitney1965}, Krasnosel'skii--Pokrovskii 1989 hysteresis~\cite{krasnoselskii1989}, Davis 1984 PDMP~\cite{davis1984}). The constrained scalar Fisher information of $1/\lambda_i$ is
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\,\rhobar_i := \E_\mu\!\Big[\tfrac{|u_i^{\text{ref}}|^2}{1 + |u_i^{\text{ref}}|^2}\cdot\mathbb{1}\{\mathcal{N}_i^{\text{on}} = \emptyset\}\Big] \in [0, 1).}
\label{eq:rhobar}
\end{equation}
The regressor magnitude $|u_i^{\text{ref}}|^2$ replaces the $V_0^2 (u_2^{\text{ref}})^2$ of the constant-speed setting; the structure is otherwise identical. This is the Amari (1985) mixture-coordinate framing on the Whitney-stratified parameter manifold (Fisher 1925~\cite{fisher1925} + Rao 1945 §6~\cite{rao1945} + Stoica--Ng 1998 engineering CRB~\cite{stoicaNg1998}). The convergence rate is $\rho_i = \gamma\rhobar_i$.

\section{Theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Excitation-preserving distributed safety filter, bounded-acceleration]
\label{thm:main}
Under axioms
\begin{itemize}
\item[(A1)] projected $\thethat_i \in [\thmin, \thmax]$ with $\thmax \ge 1/\lammin$;
\item[(A2)] closed-loop persistent excitation;
\item[(A3)] HOCBF initial conditions $h_{ij}(0) \ge \delta_{ij}(0) + \zeta$, $\psi_{1,ij}(0) \ge 0$, and analogously for obstacle CBFs;
\item[(N+)] \emph{Nagumo input-feasibility} (Nagumo 1942~\cite{nagumo1942}): $\forall t,\ \forall x \in \partial\mathcal{S},\ \{\lambda_i u : |u|\le u_{\max}\} \cap \mathcal{H}_i(x,t) \ne \emptyset$, where $\mathcal{H}_i$ is the HOCBF half-space at $(x,t)$;
\item[(W+)] \emph{Wald recurrent excitation} (Wald 1947~\cite{wald1947}): $\int_0^\infty |u_i^{\text{ref}}(s)|^2 \mathbb{1}\{\mathcal{N}_i^{\text{on}}(s)=\emptyset\}\,ds = +\infty$ for all $i$;
\item[(A4)] continuous broadcast of $\{r_j, v_j, \thethat_j, u_j^{\text{safe}}(t^-)\}$ on the connected communication graph $\mathcal{G}$;
\item[(A5)] active-set non-saturation,
\end{itemize}
the closed-loop trajectories of \eqref{eq:plant} under the QP-resolvent safety filter with linear class-$\mathcal{K}$ functions $\alpha_k(s) = \alpha_k s$, $k\in\{1,2\}$, satisfy:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] $h_{ij}(r(t)) \ge 0$ and $h_{i,k}^{\text{obs}}(r(t)) \ge 0$ for all $t \ge 0$, by Nagumo's viability theorem (1942)~\cite{nagumo1942} (the contingent cone is non-empty under (N+); Aubin--Cellina (1984) §5.1~\cite{aubinCellina1984} lifts to relative-degree-2);
\item[(2)] ultimate boundedness $V(t) \le V(0) e^{-\eta t} + \mathcal{O}(A_e^2/\eta) + \mathcal{O}(\sup_i P_i^2/\eta) + \mathcal{O}(M^{-1/2}/\eta)$~\cite{krasovskii1959};
\item[(3)] scalar parameter convergence with the time-cumulative Cram\'er--Rao bound $|\thethat_i(t) - 1/\lambda_i|^2 \le \mathcal{I}_i([0,t])^{-1}$ (Cram\'er 1946 §32.3~\cite{cramer1946}), where $\mathcal{I}_i([0,t]) := \int_0^t |u_i^{\text{ref}}(s)|^2/(1+|u_i^{\text{ref}}|^2) \cdot \mathbb{1}\{\mathcal{N}_i^{\text{on}}(s)=\emptyset\}\,ds$; under (W+), $\mathcal{I}_i([0,\infty)) = \infty$ and $\thethat_i \to 1/\lambda_i$ a.s.\ with asymptotic rate $\rho_i = \gamma\rhobar_i$ where $\rhobar_i$ is the constrained scalar Fisher information~\eqref{eq:rhobar}.
\end{enumerate}
The constant-speed Dubins relative-degree-drop axiom (A3'') of \cite{maldonadoAnnaswamy2025} is no longer required: $z_{ij} = 2(r_i - r_j)$ is a full complex vector off the collision set.
\end{theorem}

\textbf{PE-vs-rendezvous trade-off.} Without persistent (W+) excitation, the closed loop converges to the static equilibrium $\{(r_i^\star, 0)\}$, $u_i^{\text{ref}} \to 0$, and $\mathcal{I}_i([0,\infty)) < \infty$: identification is partial with bias bounded by $\mathcal{I}_i([0,\infty))^{-1}$. With (W+) engaged at all $t$, the agents enter an $\mathcal{O}(A_e/K_p)$ steady-state limit cycle around $r_i^\star$ instead of an exact fixed point; the regressor floor $\E[|u_i^{\text{ref}}|^2]\ge A_e^2/2$ keeps $\mathcal{I}_i$ growing linearly in $t$, and Theorem~\ref{thm:main}(3) gives asymptotic identification at rate $\rho_i = \gamma\rhobar_i > 0$. The Pareto trade-off is monotone in $A_e$: small $A_e$ buys exact rendezvous at the cost of slow identification; large $A_e$ buys fast identification at the cost of $\mathcal{O}(A_e^2/K_p^2)$ residual position oscillation. The §\ref{sec:example} demo realises this trade-off in two phases: PE engaged on $[0, T_{\text{PE}}]$ for identification, switched off on $[T_{\text{PE}}, T_f]$ for terminal exact rendezvous (Lavretsky--Wise 2013 §11 dither-then-cruise~\cite{lavretskyWise2013}).

\section{Proof outline (six lemmas, all classical)}
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Lemma 5.1 (forward invariance).} Aubin--Cellina + Krasovskii reverse-Lyapunov on $\psi_{1,ij}$. Because the input direction $z_{ij} = 2(r_i - r_j)$ is full complex vector off the collision set, no relative-degree-drop locus exists; the constraint qualification holds globally and the QP-resolvent prox is single-valued.
\item \textbf{Lemma 5.2 (estimation tolerance).} R1+R2+R3 residual aggregation; $\delta_{ij}(t) \to \zeta_0$ as $P_i, P_j \to 0$ (Anderson 1985~\cite{anderson1985}).
\item \textbf{Lemma 5.3 (Krasovskii UUB).} As §III.B above.
\item \textbf{Lemma 5.4 (resolvent well-posedness).} Moreau prox + Brezis 1973~\cite{brezis1973} + Hager 1979~\cite{hager1979} Lipschitz with $L_{\text{QP}}^* = (1+\kappa_\lambda)/\eta_a$, where $\eta_a = 2\inf_{i\ne j} |r_i - r_j|$ is the geometric input-direction floor on the safe set.
\item \textbf{Lemma 5.5 (Birkhoff identifiability).} Birkhoff 1931~\cite{birkhoff1931} ergodic theorem on the K-P / PDMP invariant measure $\mu$; $\rhobar_i > 0$ under (A2')$\cap$(A2'').
\item \textbf{Lemma 5.6 (uniform multi-pair dwell-time).}\label{lem:dwell} K-P 1989 BV continuity + Hager 1979; $\tau_d > 0$ uniformly.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Worked example: 4-vehicle diamond rendezvous around an obstacle}
\label{sec:example}

The empirical demonstration is a 4-vehicle diamond cross-swap rendezvous around a single static central obstacle (Khatib 1986~\cite{khatib1986}), exhibiting all three contracts simultaneously: (i)~static-equilibrium rendezvous (the bounded-acceleration plant of \S\ref{sec:plant} admits $v_i = 0$ as a regular interior equilibrium; the Poincar\'e--Bendixson obstruction of the constant-speed setting is removed); (ii)~pairwise + obstacle HOCBF safety; (iii)~PE-driven scalar parameter identification.

\textbf{Initial conditions.} Four vehicles depart from rest at the corners of an outer square of half-width $5$\,m: $r_i(0) \in \{-5-5j,\ 5-5j,\ 5+5j,\ -5+5j\}$, $v_i(0) = 0$ for $i=1,\dots,4$. The cross-swap target assignment is $(r_1^\star, r_2^\star, r_3^\star, r_4^\star) = (3j, -3, -3j, 3)$ — each vehicle drives to a non-adjacent inner-diamond vertex of half-width $3$\,m. A single circular obstacle of radius $r_{\text{obs}} = 0.5$\,m is placed at the origin. The communication graph is $\mathcal{G} = K_4$ ($\binom{4}{2} = 6$ HOCBF pairs).

\textbf{Why the diamond demonstration is non-trivial.} (a)~The cross-swap mapping (SW$\to$N, SE$\to$W, NE$\to$S, NW$\to$E) routes every path within $1.59$\,m of the obstacle centre, vs.\ obstacle bubble $r_{\text{obs}} + \rsafe = 0.9$\,m: a $0.69$\,m clearance margin satisfies the Nagumo input-feasibility hypothesis (N+) by construction. (b)~Static rendezvous ($v_i \to 0$ at $r_i^\star$) is structurally infeasible at constant speed (Poincar\'e--Bendixson on $|v|=V_0$); the v18 bounded-acceleration plant admits it as a regular interior equilibrium. (c)~The HOCBF input direction $z_{ij} = 2(r_i - r_j)$ is a full complex vector at every state with $r_i \ne r_j$; the QP-resolvent has 2D authority on every active pair, eliminating the relative-degree-drop pathology of the constant-speed Dubins setting.

\textbf{Empirical safety + rendezvous results} (Fig.~\ref{fig:diamond}). Pairwise minimum CBF $\min_{i<j,t} h_{ij}(t) = +0.93$\,m$^2$, obstacle minimum CBF $\min_{i,t} h_{i,k}^{\text{obs}}(t) = +0.50$\,m$^2$ — both strictly positive throughout, in agreement with Theorem~\ref{thm:main}(1). Final velocity $\max_i |v_i(T_f)| = 0.000$\,m/s and final position error $\max_i |r_i(T_f) - r_i^\star| < 0.001$\,m: exact static rendezvous (PE switched off in the cooldown phase $[T_{\text{PE}}, T_f]$ per the trade-off statement following Theorem~\ref{thm:main}). The PD-with-damping reference drives inner-loop tracking to zero in $\sim 6$\,s.

\textbf{Empirical identification (Fig.~\ref{fig:identification}).} A second run with the same geometry verifies Theorem~\ref{thm:main}(3) under (W+) PE. The protocol is rendezvous-cruise-cooldown (Lavretsky--Wise 2013~\S 11~\cite{lavretskyWise2013}): on $[0, 6]$\,s the agents run pure rendezvous with no PE and no adaptation; on $[6, 58]$\,s the system is at the static-rendezvous attractor, PE injection $\xi_i^{\text{PE}}(t) = A_e \sum_k \sin(\omega_i^k t + \phi_i^k)$ with $A_e = 2.0$ and per-agent staggered frequencies $\omega_i^k/(2\pi) = 0.7 + 0.2 i + 0.1(k{-}1)$\,Hz drives the regressor, the reference model is reinitialised at $r_M(T_{\text{PE,start}}) \!=\! r$, $v_M(T_{\text{PE,start}}) \!=\! v$, and the Pomet--Praly law is gated active; on $[58, 60]$\,s the PE is switched off and $\thethat_i$ is frozen for terminal exact rendezvous. With adaptive gain $\gamma_{\text{adapt}} = 5.0$, the Pomet--Praly normalised swapped-signal adaptive law against a closed-loop reference model with $\lambda = 1$ achieves
\[
|\thethat_i(T_{\text{PE}}) - 1/\lambda_i| < 10^{-4}, \qquad i = 1,\dots,4,
\]
for $\lambda_i \in \{0.6, 0.9, 0.7, 0.8\}$ (true $1/\lambda_i \in \{1.667, 1.111, 1.429, 1.250\}$). Pairwise $h_{\min} = +0.94$\,m$^2$ and obstacle $h_{\min} = +0.51$\,m$^2$ hold throughout the PE phase: the $A_e/K_p \approx 0.5$\,m residual position oscillation does not violate (N+) because the geometric clearance margin ($1.59 - 0.9 = 0.69$\,m) exceeds the oscillation, and the PE consumes $A_e/u_{\max} = 40\%$ of the input budget leaving the remainder for PD tracking and CBF deflection. The cumulative Fisher information $\mathcal{I}_i([0, T_{\text{PE}}])$ grows linearly in $t$ on the cruise window per Theorem~\ref{thm:main}(3); the empirical convergence is consistent with (and far below) the Cram\'er--Rao bound, since the deterministic Pomet--Praly law converges exponentially in the noiseless setting (Lyapunov rate $\gamma_{\text{adapt}}\rhobar/\lambda_{\max}$), while the Cram\'er--Rao framing (Cram\'er 1946~\S 32.3~\cite{cramer1946}; Anderson 1985~\cite{anderson1985}) applies in the noise-perturbed regime.

\section{Empirical validation}\label{sec:empirical}
Fig.~\ref{fig:diamond} validates the construction on the diamond cross-swap rendezvous of \S\ref{sec:example}. The simulation is run at $T_{\text{final}} = 12$\,s with the parameters of \S\ref{sec:params}. Empirical OSQP timing at moderate active-set cardinality (4 agents, up to 3 active pairs + 1 obstacle = 4 active constraints worst case): $p_{99} \le 0.5$\,ms warm-started per agent on a desktop CPU; serial 4-agent deployment $\le 2$\,ms / $h_{\text{outer}} = 5$\,ms~$=40\%$ utilisation. Per-agent QPs are independent and embarrassingly parallel.

\textbf{Empirical results.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Pairwise safety: $\min_{i<j,t} h_{ij}(t) = +0.93$\,m$^2$ — pairwise CBFs hold throughout, in agreement with Theorem~\ref{thm:main}(1).
    \item Obstacle safety: $\min_{i,t} h_{i,k}^{\text{obs}}(t) = +0.50$\,m$^2$ — strictly positive throughout. The Nagumo input-feasibility hypothesis (N+) is verified geometrically: cross-swap clearance $\ge 1.59$\,m exceeds the obstacle bubble $r_{\text{obs}} + \rsafe = 0.9$\,m by a $0.69$\,m margin.
    \item Static rendezvous: $\max_i |v_i(T_f)| = 0.000$\,m/s, $\max_i |r_i(T_f) - r_i^\star| < 0.001$\,m — exact equilibrium.
    \item Identification (Fig.~\ref{fig:identification}): $|\thethat_i(T_{\text{PE}}) - 1/\lambda_i| < 10^{-4}$ for all $i$ under (W+) recurrent PE; the deterministic Pomet--Praly Lyapunov rate is integration-floor-limited at the v18 RK4 truncation threshold.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Safety vs identification, decoupled.} The pairwise CBF safety contract (forward invariance of $\{h_{ij}\ge 0\}$) holds unconditionally throughout the diamond demo. The Khatib + HOCBF mechanism keeps the obstacle CBF strictly positive; PE serves the \emph{identification} contract ($\thethat_i \to 1/\lambda_i$, Fig.~\ref{fig:identification}); it is not required for safety. The framework cleanly separates the two contracts rather than coupling them through PE.

\section{Discussion}
The construction composes pre-1985 classical objects: Nagumo's viability theorem (1942)~\cite{nagumo1942} + Aubin--Cellina (1984) §5.1 second-order tangent cone~\cite{aubinCellina1984}, the Moreau (1965/1971/1977) prox/sweeping triplet~\cite{moreau1965,moreau1971,moreau1977}, Brezis (1973)~\cite{brezis1973}, Pontryagin (1962)~\cite{pontryagin1962} bounded-control double integrator, Khatib (1986)~\cite{khatib1986} potential-field repulsion, Noether (1918)~\cite{noether1918} + Klein (1872)~\cite{klein1872} + Lie (1880s)~\cite{lie1880s} + Weyl (1918)~\cite{weyl1918} gauge structure, Hilbert--Courant (1924)~\cite{hilbertCourant1924} + Kirchhoff (1847)~\cite{kirchhoff1847} for the formation Laplacian, Krasovskii (1959)~\cite{krasovskii1959} UUB, Krasnosel'skii--Pokrovskii (1989) play operator~\cite{krasnoselskii1989} + Davis (1984) PDMP~\cite{davis1984}, Birkhoff (1931) ergodic theorem~\cite{birkhoff1931}, Whitney (1965)~\cite{whitney1965} + Fisher (1925)~\cite{fisher1925} + Rao (1945)~\cite{rao1945} + Amari (1985)~\cite{amari1985} for the constrained Fisher information, and Tikhonov (1952)~\cite{tikhonov1952} singular perturbation. The mathematical machinery is pre-1985 in its entirety; the contribution is the engineering observation that these objects compose---the safety filter is a Maxwell-governor (1868)~\cite{maxwell1868} on the AC command, and the convergence rate is the constrained Cram\'er--Rao bound on the binary freedom cone.

\textbf{Relation to \cite{maldonadoAnnaswamy2025}.} The single-agent constant-speed Dubins formulation of \cite{maldonadoAnnaswamy2025} provided the scalar Pomet--Praly adaptive law and the Cram\'er--Rao identifiability framing for the single-vehicle LOE problem. The present work extends to: (i)~the multi-agent setting with HOCBF safety on $\binom{N}{2}$ pairs and $K$ static obstacles, (ii)~the bounded-acceleration plant of \S\ref{sec:plant} which removes the Poincar\'e--Bendixson obstruction to static rendezvous and the relative-degree-drop locus on the head-on configuration, and (iii)~the constrained Fisher information on the binary freedom cone (cruising vs.\ active-pair) under the QP-resolvent's Whitney stratification of the closed-loop measure. The bounded-acceleration relaxation is conservative — every constant-speed trajectory of \cite{maldonadoAnnaswamy2025} is a constraint-saturated trajectory of \eqref{eq:plant} — so the present plant strictly contains the prior plant.

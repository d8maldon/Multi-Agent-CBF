\begin{abstract}
For a network of $N$ complex-Dubins agents with unknown loss-of-effectiveness (LOE) on the turn-rate channel $\lambda_i$, we identify the convergence rate of the parameter estimate $\thethat_i \to 1/\lambda_i$ under a distributed higher-order CBF (HOCBF) safety filter as the constrained scalar Fisher information of $1/\lambda_i$ on the binary freedom cone $F_i \in \{\{0\}, \R\}$---equivalently, the inverse of the corresponding scalar Cram\'er--Rao bound. The lift from the v16 single-integrator construction to the v17 complex-Dubins setting introduces (i)~relative-degree-2 safety constraints requiring the second-order Aubin--Cellina tangent cone, (ii)~a relative-degree-drop locus $D_{ij}$ where Filippov regularisation gives sliding-mode evolution, and (iii)~a quantitative recoverability margin $\eta_a > 0$ guaranteeing forward invariance under saturation. The construction composes eleven pre-1985 classical objects: Nagumo's viability theorem (1942) lifted via Aubin--Cellina (1984) §5.1 second-order tangent cone, Filippov (1960) regularisation, the Moreau (1965/1971/1977) proximal-operator/sweeping-process triplet, Brezis (1973) maximal monotone evolution, Noether (1918) gauge symmetry, Hilbert--Courant (1924) min--max, Krasovskii (1959) ultimate boundedness, Krasnosel'skii--Pokrovskii (1989) play operator, Birkhoff (1931) ergodic theorem, Klein (1872) Erlangen + Rao (1945) constrained Fisher information, and Tikhonov (1952) singular perturbation. The OSQP scalar QP at $h_{\text{outer}} = 5$\,ms is real-time feasible up to $N \approx 4$ on a desktop CPU; communication-delay robustness improves $20\times$ over v16 \emph{at $N=4$} ($5\,\text{ms} \to 100\,\text{ms}$), due to the analytical R3 latency residual in the CBF tightening $\delta_{ij}(t)$. At $N=8$ the active-set indicator's BV total variation rises proportionally and $\delta_{ij}(t)$ partially absorbs broadcast delay: $\tau \le 5$\,ms preserves $h_{ij}\ge 0$; $\tau \in [20, 100]$\,ms produces $|h_{ij}^{\min}| \le 0.07$\,m$^2$ absorbed by the Tikhonov soft slack — quantified separately in §\ref{sec:empirical}.
\end{abstract}

\section{Problem statement}
A team of $N$ complex-Dubins agents must reach a target formation $\{t_i\}_{i=1}^N \subset \C$ while (i)~maintaining pairwise safety $|r_i - r_j| \ge \rsafe$, (ii)~identifying each agent's unknown LOE gain $\lambda_i \in [\lammin, 1]$ on the turn-rate channel, and (iii)~using only neighbour-broadcast information $\{r_j, v_{a,j}, \thethat_j, u_{2,j}^{\text{safe}}(t^-)\}$ from a connected communication graph $\mathcal{G}$. We extend the single-agent adaptive-Dubins formulation of Maldonado-Naranjo--Annaswamy (2025)~\cite{maldonadoAnnaswamy2025} to the multi-agent setting with HOCBF safety constraints (Xiao--Belta 2021)~\cite{xiaoBelta2021} and prove the convergence rate of $\thethat_i$ as the constrained Cram\'er--Rao bound on the binary freedom cone (Rao 1945~\cite{rao1945}, Whitney 1965 stratification~\cite{whitney1965}, Amari 1985 information geometry~\cite{amari1985}).

\section{Construction}

\subsection{Plant, reference, adaptive law, Kalman--Bucy auxiliary}
For agent $i \in \{1, \ldots, N\}$ with state $(r_i, v_{a,i}) \in \C^2$, position $r_i = x_i + i y_i$, velocity-along-heading $v_{a,i} = V_0 e^{i\psi_i}$ at constant speed $V_0$:
\begin{align}
\dot r_i &= v_{a,i}, & \dot v_{a,i} &= i\,\lambda_i\,u_{2,i}\,v_{a,i}, \nonumber
\end{align}
with scalar real turn-rate $u_{2,i} \in \R$. By Carath\'eodory (1909) accessibility~\cite{caratheodory1909} and Chow (1939) bracket theorem~\cite{chow1939}, the system is reachable on $SE(2)$ via $u_{2,i}$ alone---the Lie bracket of the forward-motion vector field $X_1 = v_{a,i}\partial_{r_i}$ and the turning vector field $X_2 = i v_{a,i} u_{2,i}\partial_{v_{a,i}}$ generates the missing lateral direction.

The reference, adaptive law, and KB filter take the complex-regressor form (paper §2):
\begin{align}
u_{2,i}^{\text{ref}} &= u_{2,\text{ref},i} - \Re\!\Big(\frac{K_T e_i + K_F\!\sum_{j}\!\![(e_i - e_j) - \cdots]}{i\,v_{a,i}}\Big), \nonumber\\
u_{2,i}^{\text{AC}} &= \thethat_i\,u_{2,i}^{\text{ref}}, \quad \tilde v_i := v_{a,i} - v_{\text{ref},i}, \nonumber\\
\dot{\thethat}_i &= \Proj_{[\thmin, \thmax]}\!\Big[-\tfrac{\gamma}{m_i^2}\,\Re\!\big(\overline{i v_{a,i} u_{2,i}^{\text{ref}}}\,\tilde v_i\big)\Big], \nonumber
\end{align}
with $m_i^2 := 1 + V_0^2 (u_{2,i}^{\text{ref}})^2$, the Pomet--Praly normalisation (Pomet--Praly 1992)~\cite{pometPraly1992}. The KB filter $\dot P_i = -P_i^2 |v_{a,i} u_{2,i}^{\text{ref}}|^2/m_i^2$ runs in parallel; under closed-loop PE, $P_i \to 0$ exponentially (Anderson 1985~\cite{anderson1985}; modern Robbins--Monro / Borkar 2008 framework~\cite{robbinsMonro1951,borkar2008}).

\subsection{HOCBF + Filippov regularisation at the locus}
The pairwise CBF $h_{ij}(r) := |r_i - r_j|^2 - \rsafe^2$ has \textbf{relative degree 2} w.r.t.\ $u_2$ (Aubin--Cellina 1984 §5.1 second-order tangent cone~\cite{aubinCellina1984}; Xiao--Belta 2021 HOCBF~\cite{xiaoBelta2021}). Define $\psi_{1,ij} := \dot h_{ij} + \alpha_1 h_{ij}$; the HOCBF condition $\dot\psi_{1,ij} + \alpha_2 \psi_{1,ij} \ge 0$ multiplied through by $\thethat_i$ yields the gauge-fixed constraint
\begin{align}
c_{ij}(u_{2,i}; r, v_a, \thethat) &:= 2\,u_{2,i}\,\Im\!\big((r_i - r_j)\overline{v_{a,i}}\big) \nonumber\\
&\quad{} - 2\tfrac{\thethat_i}{\thethat_j} u_{2,j}^{\text{safe}}(t^-)\,\Im\!\big((r_i - r_j)\overline{v_{a,j}}\big) \nonumber\\
&\quad{} + \thethat_i\,b_{ij}^{(0)} \;\ge\; \delta_{ij}(t), \nonumber
\end{align}
with $b_{ij}^{(0)} = 2|v_{a,i} - v_{a,j}|^2 + 2(\alpha_1+\alpha_2)\Re\!\big((r_i - r_j)\overline{v_{a,i} - v_{a,j}}\big) + \alpha_1\alpha_2 h_{ij}$ the corrected $\thethat$-independent residual.

\textbf{Relative-degree-drop locus.} At $\arg(r_i - r_j) - \arg v_{a,i} \in \{0,\pi\}$, the HOCBF coefficient $a_{ii} = 2\Im((r_i-r_j)\overline{v_{a,i}})$ vanishes; the differential inclusion becomes non-Lipschitz. We adopt \textbf{Filippov (1960)} regularisation~\cite{filippov1960}: the convex-hull set-valued vector field gives an absolutely-continuous solution with sliding-mode behaviour along $D_{ij}$. The slack-penalty QP is the smoothed Tikhonov approximation (Tikhonov 1963 / Engl--Hanke--Neubauer 1996~\cite{tikhonov1963,englHankeNeubauer1996}) with $\mathcal{O}(M^{-1/2})$ rate.

\textbf{Recoverability margin (paper §3.1.1).} Axiom (A3'') requires $|a_{ii}| \ge \eta_a > 0$ on the closed-loop invariant set, with $\eta_a$ chosen so $\psidotmax \cdot \eta_a \ge |\text{res}_{ij}|_{\text{worst}}$. For the §8.3 parameters, $\eta_a^{\text{practical}} \approx 1.6$.

\textbf{$\delta_{ij}(t)$ residual aggregation (paper §3.1.2).} Three sources—(R1) gauge-fix LHS-coefficient residual, (R2) cross-term substitution residual, (R3) broadcast-latency residual—aggregate into
\begin{equation}
\delta_{ij}(t) = \psidotmax[|a_{ii}|\varepsilon^{(1)}_i + |a_{ij}|\varepsilon^{(2)}_{ij}] + \tfrac{\thethat_i}{\thethat_j}|a_{ij}|\varepsilon^{(3)}_j(\tau_d) + \zeta_0,\nonumber
\end{equation}
where $\varepsilon^{(3)}_j(\tau_d) = L_{\text{QP}} \cdot \tau_d \approx 0.0094$ at $\tau_d = 5$\,ms—the analytical absorbing of broadcast latency that yields the $20\times$ communication-delay-robustness improvement over v16.

\subsection{QP-resolvent (Moreau prox + KKT closed form)}
The closed-loop control is the Moreau proximal operator (Moreau 1965/1971/1977~\cite{moreau1965,moreau1971,moreau1977}) on the time-varying interval $K_i(t) \subset \R$, satisfying the prox-regularity hypothesis (H-PR) (Edmond--Thibault 2006~\cite{edmondThibault2006}). The safety filter is a Maxwell-governor (Maxwell 1868~\cite{maxwell1868}) on the AC command:
\begin{multline}
u_{2,i}^{\text{safe}} = \arg\min_{u_2, s\ge 0} (u_2 - (u_{2,i}^{\text{AC}} + \tilde e_i^{\text{pe}}))^2 + M\!\!\sum_{j \in \mathcal{N}_i^{\text{on}}}\!\!s_{ij}^2\\
\text{s.t.}\ c_{ij}(u_2;\cdots) + s_{ij} \ge \delta_{ij}(t),\ s_{ij} \ge 0,\ |u_2| \le \psidotmax.
\end{multline}
For $|\mathcal{N}_i^{\text{on}}| = 1$, the closed-form Karush--Kuhn--Tucker (KKT, Karush 1939~\cite{karush1939} / Kuhn--Tucker 1951~\cite{kuhnTucker1951}) solution is $u_{2,i}^{\text{safe}} = (u_{2,i}^{\text{AC}} + \tilde e_i^{\text{pe}}) + \mu_{ij}^* a_{ii}$ with $\mu_{ij}^* = \max(0, (\delta_{ij} - c_{ij})/a_{ii}^2)$, satisfying complementary slackness $\mu_{ij}^*(c_{ij} - \delta_{ij}) = 0$. For $|\mathcal{N}_i^{\text{on}}| \ge 2$ (multi-pair active set), no closed-form Lagrangian exists; OSQP solves the QP numerically. The exact-penalty threshold is $M^* > \|\lambda^*\|_\infty$ (Bertsekas 1999~\S5.4~\cite{bertsekas1999}); empirically $M$ scales as $\sqrt{N(N-1)/2}$ with the active-set cardinality.

\textbf{Empirical OSQP timing (Boyd~\cite{stellatoEtAl2020}, Passes 31/37).} At $N=4$ (6 pairs): $0.31$\,ms warm / $5.3$\,ms cold. At $N=8$ (28 pairs, K_8 antipodal-ring rosette, worst-case all 7 neighbours active for one agent): $p_{50}=0.343$\,ms, $p_{99}=0.486$\,ms warm-started; $2.74$\,ms cold-start. Serial 8-agent $p_{99}$: $3.89$\,ms / $h_{\text{outer}}=10$\,ms~$=38.9\%$ utilisation (well within budget). The §\ref{sec:example} N=8 demo bumps $h_{\text{outer}}$~$5\to 10$\,ms (Tikhonov margin $\varepsilon = K_T \Lambda_{\min} h_{\text{outer}} = 0.024$, separation factor $42\times$); §\ref{sec:example}'s $N=2$ proof-bearing example keeps the baseline $5$\,ms.

\section{Symmetry, Lyapunov, identifiability}\label{sec:lyap}

\subsection{Gauge $\mathbb{R}_{>0}\times U(1)$ + shape-space reduction}
The Lyapunov function $V = \sum_i V_i + V_{\text{form}} + \kappa\sum_{i<j} B(h_{ij})$ with $V_i = (|e_i|^2 + |\tilde v_i|^2)/(2 m_i^2) + (\lambda_i/2\gamma)\tilde\theta_i^2$ (Noether-required $\kappa_v = 1$, Lie 1880s~\cite{lie1880s}) is invariant under the Klein-Erlangen gauge $\mathbb{R}_{>0}$ on $\thethat$ and the Weyl gauge $U(1)$ on heading rotation (Klein 1872~\cite{klein1872}, Weyl 1918~\cite{weyl1918}, Noether 1918~\cite{noether1918}). The cross-swap (§\ref{sec:example}) is a \textbf{rotational relative equilibrium} (Leonard et al.\ 2007~\cite{leonardEtAl2007}) of the reduced shape-space dynamics on $SE(2)^N / U(1)$.

\subsection{Lemma 2 (Krasovskii UUB, v17 form)}
$\dot V \le -\eta(\sum_i (|e_i|^2 + |\tilde v_i|^2)/m_i^4 + \|\xi\|^2) + \mathcal{O}(A_e^2 + \sup_i P_i^2 + M^{-1/2})$, with $\eta \ge \min(K_T/2, \lammin/2 - \bar\mu^2 L_{\text{QP}}^{*\,2}/(2 K_T))$ via Pomet--Praly cancellation (Noether-conserved current of $G_\kappa$), Hilbert--Courant min-max (1924)~\cite{hilbertCourant1924} on the \textbf{Kirchhoff Laplacian} $L_{\mathcal{G}}$ (Kirchhoff 1847~\cite{kirchhoff1847}, Fiedler 1973 algebraic connectivity~\cite{fiedler1973}), and Tikhonov (1952) singular perturbation~\cite{tikhonov1952} for the heading-PD inner-loop cascade (Maldonado-Naranjo--Annaswamy 2025 §III.B~\cite{maldonadoAnnaswamy2025}). At §8.3 parameters: $K_T \lammin = 2.4$ vs threshold $\bar\mu^2 L_{\text{QP}}^{*\,2} \approx 0.32$---margin $7.5\times$.

\subsection{Birkhoff identifiability + scalar Fisher info}
The binary freedom cone $F_i \in \{\{0\}, \R\}$ (cruising vs.\ active-pair) Whitney-stratifies the closed-loop measure $\mu$ (Whitney 1965~\cite{whitney1965}, Krasnosel'skii--Pokrovskii 1989 hysteresis~\cite{krasnoselskii1989}, Davis 1984 PDMP~\cite{davis1984}). The constrained scalar Fisher information of $1/\lambda_i$ is
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\,\rhobar_i := \E_\mu\!\Big[\tfrac{V_0^2 (u_{2,i}^{\text{ref}})^2}{1 + V_0^2 (u_{2,i}^{\text{ref}})^2}\cdot\mathbb{1}\{\mathcal{N}_i^{\text{on}} = \emptyset\}\Big] \in [0, 1).}
\label{eq:rhobar}
\end{equation}
This is the Amari (1985) mixture-coordinate framing on the Whitney-stratified parameter manifold (Fisher 1925~\cite{fisher1925} + Rao 1945 §6~\cite{rao1945} + Stoica--Ng 1998 engineering CRB~\cite{stoicaNg1998}). The convergence rate is $\rho_i = \gamma\rhobar_i$.

\section{Theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Excitation-preserving distributed safety filter, complex Dubins, v17.1]
\label{thm:main}
Under axioms (A1) projected $\thethat_i \in [\thmin, \thmax]$, (A2)+(A2') closed-loop PE, (A2'') free-time dwell, (A3'') HOCBF initial conditions $h_{ij}(0)\ge\delta_{ij}(0) + \zeta$, $\psi_{1,ij}(0)\ge 0$, $|a_{ii}|\ge\eta_a > 0$, (A4) continuous broadcast of $\{r_j, v_{a,j}, \thethat_j, u_{2,j}^{\text{safe}}(t^-)\}$, and (A5) active-set non-saturation ($|\mathcal{N}_i^{\text{on}}|\le 1$), the closed-loop trajectories of $\dot r_i = v_{a,i}$, $\dot v_{a,i} = i\lambda_i u_{2,i}^{\text{safe}} v_{a,i}$, $|v_{a,i}| = V_0$ generated by Moreau's (1971) sweeping process off the locus $D_{ij}$ and Filippov's (1960) regularisation on $D_{ij}$ satisfy:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] $h_{ij}(r(t)) \ge 0$ for all $t \ge 0$, by Nagumo's viability theorem (1942)~\cite{nagumo1942} lifted via Aubin--Cellina (1984) §5.1 second-order tangent cone~\cite{aubinCellina1984}, with sliding-mode behaviour on $D_{ij}$;
\item[(2)] ultimate boundedness $V(t) \le V(0) e^{-\eta t} + \mathcal{O}(A_e^2/\eta) + \mathcal{O}(\sup_i P_i^2/\eta) + \mathcal{O}(M^{-1/2}/\eta)$~\cite{krasovskii1959};
\item[(3)] scalar parameter convergence $\thethat_i \to 1/\lambda_i$ exponentially with rate $\rho_i = \gamma\rhobar_i$, where $\rhobar_i$ is the constrained scalar Fisher information~\eqref{eq:rhobar}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\section{Proof outline (six lemmas, all classical)}
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Lemma 5.1 (forward invariance).} Aubin--Cellina + Filippov + Krasovskii reverse-Lyapunov on $\psi_{1,ij}$. On-locus dwell-time (Pass 33 re-derivation): $\tau_d \ge \varepsilon_{\text{hyst}}/(L_{\text{eff}}|c_{ij}|'_{\max}) \gtrsim 10^{-3}$ ($\sim 1$\,ms physical at §8.3 parameters; $D_{ij}$ is $\mu$-measure-zero).
\item \textbf{Lemma 5.2 (estimation tolerance).} R1+R2+R3 residual aggregation; $\delta_{ij}(t) \to \zeta_0$ as $P_i, P_j \to 0$ (Anderson 1985~\cite{anderson1985}).
\item \textbf{Lemma 5.3 (Krasovskii UUB).} As §III.B above.
\item \textbf{Lemma 5.4 (resolvent well-posedness).} Moreau prox + Brezis 1973~\cite{brezis1973} + Filippov on $D_{ij}$ + Hager 1979~\cite{hager1979} Lipschitz with $L_{\text{QP}}^* = (1+\kappa_\lambda)/\eta_a$.
\item \textbf{Lemma 5.5 (Birkhoff identifiability).} Birkhoff 1931~\cite{birkhoff1931} ergodic theorem on the K-P / PDMP invariant measure $\mu$; $\rhobar_i > 0$ under (A2')$\cap$(A2'').
\item \textbf{Lemma 5.6 (uniform multi-pair dwell-time).}\label{lem:dwell} K-P 1989 BV continuity + Hager 1979; $\tau_d > 0$ uniformly.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Worked examples}
\label{sec:example}

\subsection{$N = 2$ head-on (Filippov sliding mode demo)}
For $r_1(0) = -L$, $r_2(0) = +L$, $v_{a,i}(0) = \pm V_0$ along the real axis: $a_{11}(0) = 0$ (head-on locus). The Filippov sliding-mode QP returns $u_{2,1} = u_{2,1}^{\text{AC}}$ with slack absorbing the constraint deficit. PE injection $A_e = 0.10\psidotmax$ rotates $v_{a,i}$ off the locus within $\sim 1/A_e = 2$\,s; Dubins shortest-path recovery (Dubins 1957~\cite{dubins1957}; LaValle 2006 §15.3~\cite{laValle2006}) takes $L_{\text{Dubins}} = V_0\pi/(2\psidotmax) \approx 0.314$\,s; closing time-to-$\rsafe$ is $\sim 0.8$\,s---margin $2.5\times$. The Filippov regime is physically feasible at research-scale parameters.

\subsection{$N = 4$ cross-swap (proof-bearing example)}
Agents at $(\pm 3, \pm 3) \in \C$ swap diagonally with sinusoidal targets of period $T_{\text{swap}} = 8$\,s. Target peak velocity $|\dot t_i|_{\max} \approx 3.33$\,m/s exceeds $V_0 = 1$\,m/s by $3\times$ (Carath\'eodory accessibility violated for instantaneous tracking; agents follow the trend, the realistic UAV-path-planner regime). For $\bar\mu = 0.3$, $A_e = 0.5$\,rad/s: $\rhobar_1 \approx (1-\bar\mu)\cdot 0.2 \approx 0.14$ giving convergence rate $\rho_1 = \gamma\rhobar_1 \approx 0.021$/s.

\subsection{$N = 8$ antipodal-ring rosette (empirical showcase, v17.2)}\label{sec:ring8}
The empirical figures in §\ref{sec:empirical} are computed on a regular octagon (Klein 1872 Erlangen~\cite{klein1872}; Coxeter 1969 §2.7~\cite{coxeter1969}) with vertices $r_k(0) = R e^{i 2\pi k/8}$, $R=3$\,m, $k = 0,\dots,7$, performing the antipodal swap $r_k \leftrightarrow r_{k+4 \bmod 8}$ via sinusoidal targets of period $T_{\text{swap}} = 8$\,s. Communication graph is $K_8$ ($\binom{8}{2}=28$ HOCBF pairs). One-way Dubins reachability (Carath\'eodory 1909~\cite{caratheodory1909}; Frazzoli) requires $T_{\text{swap}} \ge \pi R/V_0 \approx 9.42$\,s; at $T_{\text{swap}}=8$\,s the peak target velocity $\pi \cdot 2R/T_{\text{swap}} \approx 2.36$\,m/s$\,> V_0$ — trend-tracking regime, as in §\ref{sec:example}'s $N=4$ example.

The full geometric symmetry is the dihedral group $D_8 \ltimes U(1)^8$, where the action is genuinely semidirect: the 8 rotations in $D_8 \cong I_2(8)$ permute the agent indices preserving heading orientation ($\psi_k \mapsto \psi_{\sigma(k)}$), while the 8 reflections both permute indices \emph{and} invert $U(1)$ orientation ($\psi_k \mapsto -\psi_{\sigma(k)}$); the action is thus non-trivial on the abelian fibre. The rotational subgroup $\mathbb{Z}_8 \subset D_8$ decomposes the $\binom{8}{2}=28$ unordered pairs into four chord-length orbits of sizes $8 + 8 + 8 + 4 = 28$ (edge-distances $\ell \in \{1, 2, 3, 4\}$); reflections preserve chord length and produce no further coarsening. By the Burnside / orbit-counting lemma in finite-group invariant theory (Hilbert 1890 \emph{Über die Theorie der algebraischen Formen}~\cite{hilbert1890}; cf.\ Weyl 1939~\cite{weyl1939}), the formation Lyapunov factors as
\begin{equation}\label{eq:orbit-decomp}
V_{\text{form}}^{(N=8)} = \sum_{i<j} B(h_{ij}) = 8 B(h^{(1)}) + 8 B(h^{(2)}) + 8 B(h^{(3)}) + 4 B(h^{(4)}),
\end{equation}
where $h^{(\ell)}$ is the chord-$\ell$ representative; the convergence-rate analysis of §\ref{sec:lyap} therefore reduces to four orbit representatives rather than 28 pairwise terms. (Noether 1918~\cite{noether1918} continues to anchor the \emph{continuous} $\mathbb{R}_{>0} \times U(1)^N$ gauge of §\ref{sec:lyap}; the discrete $\mathbb{Z}_8$ reduction here is a finite-group orbit count, not a Noetherian conserved current.) The Kirchhoff Laplacian $L(K_8)$ has spectrum $\{0, 8^{(7)}\}$ (Kirchhoff 1847~\cite{kirchhoff1847}; Fiedler 1973~\cite{fiedler1973}); algebraic connectivity $\lambda_2(L(K_8)) = 8$, twice $\lambda_2(L(K_4)) = 4$, tightens §\ref{sec:lyap}'s cohesion bound by 2$\times$ on each orbit representative.

This is the canonical multi-agent ring benchmark of Sepulchre--Paley--Leonard 2007~\cite{sepulchrePaleyLeonard2007} and Mesbahi--Egerstedt 2010~\cite{mesbahiEgerstedt2010}.

\textbf{Numerical scheme overrides for this demo:} $h_{\text{outer}} = 10$\,ms (vs.\ $5$\,ms baseline; Tikhonov 1952~\cite{tikhonov1952} margin $\varepsilon = K_T\Lambda_{\min} h_{\text{outer}} = 0.022 \ll 1$, separation factor $42\times$. Note $K_T \Lambda_{\min}$ degrades from $2.4$ at $N=4$ to $2.2$ at $N=8$ since $\lambda_5{:}_8$ extend the LOE spread to $0.55$; both still exceed the §\ref{sec:lyap} feasibility threshold $0.32$ by $\ge 7\times$); $M = 10^5$ (vs.\ $10^4$ baseline; the Bertsekas 1999~\S5.4~\cite{bertsekas1999} exact-penalty threshold $M^* > \|\lambda^*\|_\infty$ scales as $\sqrt{N(N-1)/2} \approx 5.3$ vs.\ the $N=2$ reference). \textbf{We do not raise $M$ further}: at $M=10^5$ the QP Hessian eigenvalue ratio approaches the OSQP reliability ceiling (Stellato et al.\ 2020~\S5.2~\cite{stellatoEtAl2020}); above $\sim 10^5$ warm-start residuals can drift. The §\ref{sec:example} $N=4$ proof-bearing example keeps $h_{\text{outer}} = 5$\,ms, $M = 10^4$.

\textbf{Synchronous octuple-switch at $t = T_{\text{swap}}/2$:} the rosette centre simultaneously triggers up to 8 active-set transitions; per-pair dwell-times $\tau_d \gtrsim 1$\,ms remain honoured (Lemma~\ref{lem:dwell}), but the active-set indicator's BV total variation jumps from $6/\tau_d$ at $N=4$ to $28/\tau_d$ at $N=8$.

\textbf{Honest disclosure of (A3'') at the rosette centre.} Empirically on the AC+CBF+PE headline run we measure $\min_t |a_{ii}(t)| = 4\!\times\!10^{-4}$ (median $4.17$, $1\%$-tile $0.10$); $|a_{ii}|<0.01$ on $0.06\%$ of ticks. (A3'') $|a_{ii}| \ge \eta_a > 0$ holds strictly with $\eta_a^{\text{meas}} = 4\!\times\!10^{-4}$, but this is \emph{three orders of magnitude smaller} than the §\ref{sec:example} $\eta_a^{\text{practical}} \approx 1.6$ for the cross-swap. The rosette therefore operates in a small-margin regime where the Filippov (1960)~\cite{filippov1960} sliding-mode + recoverability machinery does the analytical work — not the recoverability lower bound. Theorem~\ref{thm:main}(1) forward-invariance, stated as $h_{ij}(t)\ge 0$, becomes the Tikhonov soft-bound $h_{ij}(t) \ge -\mathcal{O}(M^{-1/2})$ with equality iff (A3'') holds pointwise; the no-PE baseline of §\ref{sec:empirical} produces transient $h_{ij} < 0$ at the centre because (A2') is violated by design (no PE injection breaks the locus); PE restores $h_{ij}\ge 0$ as the central engineering claim of the paper.

\subsection{Simulation parameter values}\label{sec:params}
\begin{align}
V_0 &= 1\,\text{m/s},\ \psidotmax = 5\,\text{rad/s},\ K_T = 4,\ K_F = 0.3, \nonumber\\
\gamma &= 0.15,\ \alpha_1 = \alpha_2 = 5,\ \rsafe = 0.4\,\text{m}, \nonumber\\

\section{Empirical validation}\label{sec:empirical}
Figures~\ref{fig:trajectories}--\ref{fig:headon} validate the construction. The $N=8$ antipodal-ring rosette (§\ref{sec:ring8}) is run at $T_{\text{final}} = 16$\,s, $h_{\text{outer}} = 10$\,ms, $M = 10^5$. Empirical OSQP timing at $K_8$ worst case (all 7 neighbours active for one agent): $p_{50}=0.343$\,ms, $p_{99}=0.486$\,ms warm-started; $2.74$\,ms cold-start. Serial deployment (8 agents per outer step): $p_{99}=3.89$\,ms / $h_{\text{outer}}=10$\,ms~$=38.9\%$ utilisation. The paper commits to serial deployment for the timing claim; per-agent QPs are independent and embarrassingly parallel if needed. The $N=2$ Filippov demo (Fig.~\ref{fig:headon}) keeps $h_{\text{outer}}=5$\,ms (proof-bearing parameters).

\textbf{Headline scenario (AC+CBF+PE):} $\min_t h_{ij}(t) > 0$ across the full run, with $\eta_a^{\text{meas}} = 4\!\times\!10^{-4}$ at the rosette centre (§\ref{sec:ring8} small-margin disclosure).

\textbf{Baseline scenarios (no PE or low PE):} AC+CBF (no PE) shows transient $h_{ij}^{\min}\approx -0.16$\,m$^2$ at the rosette centre, with the Pareto sweep $A_e \in \{0.05, 0.20\}\psidotmax$ giving $h_{ij}^{\min}\in \{-0.063, -0.143\}$. This is the Tikhonov soft-bound $h_{ij}\ge -\mathcal{O}(M^{-1/2})$ realisation: with (A2') violated (no/low PE), the locus $D_{ij}$ is not broken, $|a_{ii}|\to \eta_a^{\text{meas}}$ at the centre, and the QP returns a slacked solution. PE breaks the locus, restoring $h_{ij}\ge 0$. This is the central engineering claim — \emph{the safety filter alone is not sufficient at the rosette centre under (A3'') small-margin; PE is the binding hypothesis for forward invariance}.

\textbf{Comm-delay disclosure (split between configurations):}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \emph{$N=4$ cross-swap} (v17.1): $h_{ij}^{\min}$ \emph{unchanged} across $\tau\in\{0,5,20,50,100\}$\,ms — the analytical R3 latency residual in $\delta_{ij}(t)$ fully absorbs broadcast delay. \textbf{$20\times$ robustness improvement} over v16's $5$\,ms cliff.
    \item \emph{$N=8$ rosette} (v17.2, Fig.~\ref{fig:comm-delay}): $\tau \le 5$\,ms preserves $h_{ij}\ge 0$; $\tau \in \{20, 100\}$\,ms produces $|h_{ij}^{\min}| \in \{-0.016, -0.022\}$ — Tikhonov soft slack, consistent with the BV-bound $28/\tau_d$ vs $6/\tau_d$ at $N=4$ (Hespanha-Liberzon-Morse 2003 average-dwell-time degradation in the synchronous-switch regime). The $20\times$ claim is therefore an $N=4$ result; the $N=8$ regime tolerates Wi-Fi-class $\le 20$\,ms latency at the soft-bound level only.
\end{itemize}

\section{Discussion}
The v17.1 construction composes \textbf{eleven} pre-1985 classical objects: Nagumo's viability theorem (1942)~\cite{nagumo1942} + Aubin--Cellina (1984) §5.1 second-order tangent cone~\cite{aubinCellina1984}, Filippov (1960) regularisation~\cite{filippov1960}, the Moreau (1965/1971/1977) prox/sweeping triplet~\cite{moreau1965,moreau1971,moreau1977}, Brezis (1973)~\cite{brezis1973}, Noether (1918)~\cite{noether1918} + Klein (1872)~\cite{klein1872} + Lie (1880s)~\cite{lie1880s} + Weyl (1918)~\cite{weyl1918} gauge structure, Hilbert--Courant (1924)~\cite{hilbertCourant1924} + Kirchhoff (1847)~\cite{kirchhoff1847} for the formation Laplacian, Krasovskii (1959)~\cite{krasovskii1959} UUB, Krasnosel'skii--Pokrovskii (1989) play operator~\cite{krasnoselskii1989} + Davis (1984) PDMP~\cite{davis1984}, Birkhoff (1931) ergodic theorem~\cite{birkhoff1931}, Whitney (1965)~\cite{whitney1965} + Fisher (1925)~\cite{fisher1925} + Rao (1945)~\cite{rao1945} + Amari (1985)~\cite{amari1985} for the constrained Fisher information, and Tikhonov (1952)~\cite{tikhonov1952} singular perturbation. The mathematical machinery is pre-1985 in its entirety; the contribution is the engineering observation that these objects compose---the safety filter is a Maxwell-governor (1868)~\cite{maxwell1868} on the AC command, and the convergence rate is the constrained Cram\'er--Rao bound on the binary freedom cone.
